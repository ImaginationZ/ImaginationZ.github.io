Research Experiences

Computer Vision

2014 - 2015

Research Assistant

at Brain-like Computing and Machine Intelligence Lab, Shanghai Jiaotong University
Advisor: Prof. Hongtao Lu

I have been reading materials on Stereo Vision and Optical Flow.
Stereo Vision recovers depth information from a pair of images of the same scene, while Optical Flow tracks the movement of pixels in adjacent video frames. Both of them call for pixel-level Image Matching to produce correspondence information.
Recently I am working on using rotational patches to improve Deep Matching, while it is observed that local rotating transformation is hard to catch in many approaches based on patches or segmentation. It is due to the basic part of aggregation in cost function, the patch, is hard to maintain local shape under rotating transformation. As following the approach of building divide-and-conquer translation layers, I suggest another layers of rotation, when any part of image is transformed under both degrees.
